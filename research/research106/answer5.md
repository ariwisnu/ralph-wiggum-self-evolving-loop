## Reasoning

Let me work through this riddle clue by clue:

1. "I hang from the roof in winter" - Something that forms and dangles from a roof or eaves during cold weather. This points to something that forms in freezing conditions.

2. "the warmer the day gets, the longer I grow" - This is the key clue. As temperatures rise above freezing during the day, this thing grows longer. This happens because snow or ice on the roof partially melts, water drips down, and refreezes at the tip as it gets colder, extending it. The melting-dripping-refreezing cycle makes it grow.

3. Putting it together: A spike of ice that hangs from the edge of a roof, formed by dripping water that freezes. When the day warms up enough to melt the snow/ice above, water trickles down and the structure grows longer.

This is the classic description of an icicle. Icicles form when water from melting snow or ice runs down and refreezes at the tip, growing longer during periods of slight warming (above freezing during the day, freezing at night or at the dripping point).
