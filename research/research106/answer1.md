## Reasoning

Let me work through this riddle clue by clue:

1. "I hang from the roof in winter" - Something that forms and hangs down from the edge of a roof during cold weather. This strongly suggests ice formation, specifically something that dangles from roof edges, gutters, or eaves in winter.

2. "the warmer the day gets, the longer I grow" - This is the key clue. When it warms up slightly (but stays around or below freezing), snow and ice on the roof partially melt. The meltwater drips down and refreezes at the tip as it falls, adding length. So warmer (above-freezing) days cause more melting/dripping, which makes this thing grow longer.

3. Combining these: something that hangs from the roof in winter and grows longer as it gets warmer (because warmth causes melting water to drip and refreeze, extending it downward).

This perfectly describes an icicle. Icicles form when snow or ice on a roof melts during warmer parts of the day, and the dripping water refreezes into a hanging, tapering spike of ice. The warmer the day (up to the melting point), the more water drips and refreezes, making the icicle grow longer.

## Final Answer

An icicle.
