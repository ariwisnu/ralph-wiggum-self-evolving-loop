## Reasoning

Let me work through this riddle line by line:

1. "I hang from the roof in winter" - This is something that forms on the edge or eaves of a roof during cold weather. Several things hang from roofs in winter, but the key clue is the next line.

2. "the warmer the day gets, the longer I grow" - This is the critical, slightly counterintuitive clue. Most things shrink or disappear in warmth. But this object grows when it gets warmer.

3. Putting it together: An icicle forms when snow or ice on a roof melts slightly during a warmer part of the day, and the dripping water refreezes at the tip. The warmth causes melting, which feeds water down to the tip of the icicle, making it grow longer. (It only fully melts and falls when it gets too warm, but on a cold-but-slightly-warming day, the partial melting elongates it.)

The classic phrasing of this riddle ("the warmer the day, the longer I grow") is a well-known characteristic of icicles - the daytime sun melts the snow/ice above, water trickles down and refreezes at the bottom tip, lengthening the icicle.
