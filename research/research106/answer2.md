## Reasoning

Let me work through the clues one by one:

1. "I hang from the roof in winter" - This suggests something that forms on the edge or eaves of a roof during cold weather. Ice and snow accumulate on roofs in winter.

This describes an icicle perfectly. Icicles form when snow or ice on a roof melts in slightly warmer temperatures, the meltwater runs down to the edge, and then refreezes as it drips, extending the icicle downward. The warmer-but-still-freezing conditions (sun warming the roof while air stays cold) cause the icicle to lengthen.

The riddle is a classic personification: the icicle "hangs from the roof" and "grows longer" as melting provides more water to freeze at its tip.
