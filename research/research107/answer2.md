## Reasoning

Let me break this riddle down line by line.

1. **"I am always hungry and must always be fed"** — Something that constantly consumes fuel to keep going. This strongly points to **fire**, which must be fed wood, oxygen, or other fuel or it dies out.

2. **"the finger I touch will soon turn red"** — Touching it burns you, and the skin turns red. Again, this is the classic description of **fire**. These first two lines are, almost word for word, the traditional "What am I?" riddle whose answer is fire.

3. **"yet the farther you stand from me, the warmer you feel"** — Here the riddle flips the expected logic. With a normal fire, the *closer* you stand, the *warmer* you feel; the farther away you go, the *colder* you get. The word "yet" deliberately signals a contradiction. So this third line is physically backwards for fire (and for any ordinary heat source).

This is the catch. The first two lines set up the familiar "fire" answer, but the third line states the opposite of how heat actually behaves. No real object becomes warmer to you as you move away from it — radiant heat decreases with distance. So:

- If I take the riddle as a slightly modified/trick version of the classic fire riddle, the intended answer is still **fire**, and the third line is an inverted (incorrect) clause designed to trip up a careless reader.
- If I take all three lines as strict requirements, there is **no consistent real-world object** that satisfies them, because "the farther you stand, the warmer you feel" contradicts the burning, fuel-hungry heat source described in lines 1–2.

The most reasonable interpretation is that this is the well-known fire riddle with a deliberately reversed final line. The honest reading: lines 1 and 2 describe fire, but line 3 is inverted and does not actually hold for fire.

## Final Answer

**Fire.** The first two lines ("always hungry / must be fed" and "the finger I touch will turn red") are the classic riddle for fire. However, the third line is logically backwards: with fire you feel *warmer* the *closer* you stand, not farther away. So while the intended answer is fire, that final clause contradicts fire's real behavior — meaning no actual object truly satisfies all three lines as written. Best answer: **fire**, with the note that the "farther you stand, the warmer you feel" line is inverted/incorrect.
