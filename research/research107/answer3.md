## Reasoning

Let me break this riddle into its individual clues:

1. **"I am always hungry and must always be fed"** — This describes something that consumes fuel continuously to keep going. The classic answer here is **fire**, which "eats" wood, oxygen, or other fuel and dies out if not fed.

2. **"The finger I touch will soon turn red"** — Touching fire burns your skin, leaving it red (or worse). This again points squarely at **fire**. These first two lines are nearly word-for-word the famous traditional "fire" riddle: *"I am always hungry, I must always be fed. The hand that touches me will soon turn red."*

3. **"Yet the farther you stand from me, the warmer you feel"** — Here is the twist. This clue is the *opposite* of how fire actually behaves. With a real fire, the **closer** you stand, the warmer you feel; the farther away you move, the **colder** you get. So this line directly contradicts the fire interpretation set up by the first two lines.

So I notice something important: this is a modified/inverted version of the classic fire riddle. The first two lines are textbook "fire," but the third line has been flipped to contradict fire's actual physics. There is no real-world object that:
- needs constant feeding (fire-like),
- burns/reddens skin on contact (fire-like),
- and yet radiates *more* warmth the farther away you are (anti-fire / physically impossible).

That combination is self-contradictory. The riddle reads like the well-known fire riddle with a deliberately reversed final clause.

Given that, the honest interpretation is: the intended/expected answer based on the dominant, well-known clues is **fire**, but the final line is an inverted clue that breaks the consistency. If forced to name what the riddle is "supposed" to be, it is fire. If taken literally, no single object satisfies all three lines at once because line 3 is the reverse of fire's behavior.

## Final Answer

**Fire.**

The first two lines are the classic "fire" riddle word-for-word (always hungry/must be fed, touch turns the finger red). However, the third line ("the farther you stand from me, the warmer you feel") is inverted and contradicts how fire actually works — with fire you feel warmer the *closer* you are, not the farther. So the intended answer is fire, but that final clue is a deliberate reversal that makes the riddle internally inconsistent rather than describing any real object.
