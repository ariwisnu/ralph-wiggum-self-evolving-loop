## Reasoning

Let me work through each line of the riddle.

2. "the finger I touch will soon turn red" — Touching it causes a burn (redness). Again, this strongly points to fire. These first two lines are, almost word-for-word, the well-known children's riddle whose answer is **fire**.
3. "yet the farther you stand from me, the warmer you feel" — Here is where things break down. For fire (or any heat source), the relationship is the opposite of what's stated: the *closer* you stand, the warmer you feel; the *farther* away, the cooler. So this third line directly contradicts the physics implied by the first two lines.

So I notice a deliberate inversion. The first two clues unambiguously describe fire, but the third clue states a backwards/impossible relationship. No real object satisfies all three lines simultaneously:
- Fire satisfies lines 1 and 2 but flatly violates line 3.
- I can't think of anything that is "hungry/must be fed," "burns on touch," AND "feels warmer the farther away you are" — that last property describes essentially nothing physical (heat intensity falls off with distance, it does not grow).

The most reasonable read is that this is the classic "fire" riddle with the final line altered to insert a contradiction (the word "farther" where the original logic requires "closer"). The intended/underlying answer the riddle is gesturing at is fire, but the third line as written is logically inconsistent with that answer.

## Final Answer

The riddle is built on the classic **fire** riddle ("always hungry, must be fed; the finger I touch turns red" → fire). So: the intended answer is **fire**, but I'd flag that the final line ("the farther you stand from me, the warmer you feel") is backwards — it should be "the closer you stand." Taken literally, the riddle has no valid solution because that clue contradicts the first two.
