## Reasoning

Let me work through the clues one at a time.

1. "I have no mouth, but I repeat every word you say." — Something that reproduces your speech without being alive. This is a hallmark of an echo: a reflection of sound that repeats whatever you say back to you.

2. "I have no body, but I answer you from far away." — An echo is intangible (no body) and comes back to you across a distance, as when you shout in a canyon, a cave, or across a valley and hear the sound return.

3. "And the farther you move from me, the louder my reply." — This is the odd line. In real acoustics, an echo gets *fainter* (and arrives with a longer delay) the farther you are from the reflecting surface, not louder. So taken literally, this clue contradicts the physics of a normal echo.
