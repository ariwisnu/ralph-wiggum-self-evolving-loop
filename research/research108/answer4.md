## Reasoning

Let me work through this riddle line by line.

1. "I have no mouth, but I repeat every word you say." - Something that has no physical form yet reproduces your speech. This is the classic signature of an **echo**: it lacks a mouth but still "speaks" your words back.

2. "I have no body, but I answer you from far away." - No physical body, yet it responds to you across a distance. Again, this fits an echo perfectly. An echo is sound reflecting off a distant surface (a cliff, canyon wall, mountain), so the "reply" seems to come from far off.

3. "And the farther you move from me, the louder my reply." - This is the unusual, almost paradoxical line. In real physics, an echo actually gets *quieter* (and arrives later) as you move farther from the reflecting surface, because the sound has to travel a longer round-trip and spreads out. So this line is not physically accurate for a true echo.

So there are two ways to read the riddle:

- The intended answer is **an echo**, and the third line is just poetic license / a playful exaggeration that doesn't match real acoustics. The first two lines so strongly fingerprint an echo that this is almost certainly the answer the riddle is reaching for.

- If we take the third line literally and demand strict accuracy, no real echo behaves that way, which makes the riddle internally inconsistent. But riddles routinely bend reality for effect, and there isn't a better single object that "repeats your words" and "answers from far away" the way an echo does.

Weighing both, the overwhelming weight of the imagery (no mouth, no body, repeats your words, replies from a distance) points to an echo. The "louder the farther" line is best read as creative embellishment rather than a literal physical property.

## Final Answer

**An echo.**

The first two lines describe an echo exactly (no mouth or body, yet it repeats your words and answers from a distance). The third line is poetic exaggeration that doesn't match real acoustics, since a real echo actually grows fainter with distance, not louder, but the answer the riddle is pointing to is still an echo.
