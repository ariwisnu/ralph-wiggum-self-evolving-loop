## Reasoning

Let me work through this riddle line by line.

1. "I have no mouth, but I repeat every word you say." — Something that has no mouth yet reproduces your speech. This is the classic signature of an **echo**: it gives back your own words without any voice or mouth of its own.

2. "I have no body, but I answer you from far away." — An echo is not a physical being; it is sound reflecting off a distant surface (a cliff, canyon wall, large building, or empty hall). The reply seems to come from "over there," far from you.

3. "And the farther you move from me, the louder my reply." — This line is the twist that makes the riddle interesting. With a true sound echo, distance usually makes the reflection *fainter*, not louder. But interpreted poetically, it points to a real property of echoes: if you stand too close to a reflecting surface, the returning sound arrives almost instantly and merges with your own voice, so you hear no distinct echo at all. You need enough distance (roughly 17+ meters) for the reflection to separate from your voice and become clearly audible — so "the farther you move, the more distinct/clear my reply." It can also be read as: the farther away you are, the louder you have to shout, and the louder the echo comes back.

Lines 1 and 2 are an almost word-for-word match to the traditional echo riddle ("I speak without a mouth and hear without ears..."). Line 3 is a slight stylistic inversion, but the core identity of the answer doesn't change. Nothing else fits "repeats your words" + "no mouth/body" + "answers from far away" as cleanly as an echo.

## Final Answer

An **echo**.
